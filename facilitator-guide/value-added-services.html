Intro

Purpose

Teach Mobile Associates (MAs) about our Value Added Services and how to offer them to customers based on each customer’s individual needs.

After successfully completing this module, MAs will be able to:

  • Describe T-Mobile’s Value Added Services.
  • Match Value Added Services with customer needs and lifestyle.
  • Recommend Value Added Services as part of combined pricing.

Explain

  • Nearly every customer can benefit from some type of service in addition to what is included in their service plan.
  • We are not in the business of offering cookie-cutter recommendations or forcing a sale. We Make It Personal!
  • We make customized recommendations for which additional services will benefit customers by asking personalized lifestyle questions.
  • Some of the additional services are free. Others have additional costs or monthly fees.
  • Combined pricing is an effective way to Guide The Purchase, by combining several products or services into one overall price.

Dos & Don’ts

  • It’s usually a customer’s first instinct to say no to anything that costs extra.
  • Be upfront and explain the breakdown of the total price. Ensure you match the pace of your customer.
  • Keep things simple, by starting with the combined price and explaining everything that’s included instead of starting with the lowest price and adding charges.
  • Remember, we are not trying to trick anyone—we’re always about right fitting the customer.

Do the Math Activity

Purpose

Get Mobile Associates thinking about how to quickly combine pricing.

Instructions

  1. Present learners with rate plan/services combos.
  2. Do the math to come up with the combined price.
  3. Let the class come up with a couple of their own examples.

Example Combined Pricing:

  • Individual T-Mobile ONE + Stateside International (no auto-pay):
    • $75 + $15 = $90
  • T-Mobile ONE Family Plan (2 lines w/auto-pay) + Name ID:
    • $120 + $4 = $124

Bring It Together Activity

Purpose

Give MAs an opportunity to practice asking quality, open-ended questions to learn more about the customer’s lifestyle in order to recommend Value Added Services.

Explain

  • Based on the information collected, we’re going to practice recommending a combined price solution, including a rate plan and additional services.
  • A common mistake is recommending a product or service before we truly know if that fits our customer’s needs, so make sure you understand enough about your customer before making a recommendation.

Instructions

TIME: 25 min Skill Practice (about 5 min each), 15 min debrief

Split class into small groups of 3-4. Group maximum is 4, to allow everyone time to Skill Practice.

Instruct learners to:

  1. Practice asking lifestyle questions of each other to learn about them and how they may use their wireless phone. Focus on using open-ended questions, like:
    • What does a typical day look like for you?
    • How do you use your phone throughout the day?
    • How often do you travel and where?
  2. Once they’ve gathered enough information, they should offer a personalized recommendation with combined pricing, including:
    • Rate plan
    • Value Added Service (with combined pricing)
    • Why they believe this recommendation will benefit them
  3. Using Customer Connect, learners should create a Purchase Estimate based on the recommendation and email it to their partner.

Rotate until each learner has been the Mobile Expert, customer and observer.

Debrief

  • What went well?
  • What was challenging?
  • How did you feel using Customer Connect during the Skill Practice?
  • How did you use the Interaction Model?
  • What open-ended questions worked well for you?
  • Why don’t close-ended questions work well for discovering a customer’s needs?
