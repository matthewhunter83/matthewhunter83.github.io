Intro

Purpose

Mobile Associates (MAs) will be given time to explore T-Mobile’s line-up of tablets, wearables and mobile hotspot devices, along with their corresponding rate plans.

After successfully completing this module, they will be able to:

  • Describe the benefits of tablets.
  • Describe the benefits of wearables.
  • Identify which products can use DIGITS.
  • Identify corresponding rate plans for these various devices.

Ask

  • Show of hands: Who owns a tablet?
  • Show of hands: Who owns a wearable like an Apple Watch or Samsung Gear?
  • Anyone ever use a mobile hotspot? How and where did you use it?

Explain

  • Everyone loves a new phone, but we have so many other devices to get excited about!
  • Tablets, wearables, and mobile hotspots take mobile connectivity to the next level.
  • While it’s important to learn about these products and how they can benefit customers, it’s also important to understand the type of mobile internet rate plan required for these devices to access our network.
  • And let’s not forget about DIGITS!
  • If you’re talking tablets and wearables, then you should be talking DIGITS too.

DIY Activity

Instructions

TIME: 10 min setup, 60 min research, 20 min debrief

Research mobile internet devices, rate plans, and selling strategies.

  • Devices (https://c2.t-mobile.com/community/devices), filter for Tablets, Wearables, and Hotspots
  • T-Mobile ONE Tablet: Postpaid (TI) (425002)
  • T-Mobile ONE Tablet: No Credit Check (TI) (428569)
  • T-Mobile Essentials Tablet: Postpaid (TE) (442968)
  • T-Mobile Essentials Tablet: No Credit Check (TE) (442967)
  • Data with paired DIGITS plans (431515)
  • Mobile Internet for hotspots: Postpaid (TI) (429651)
  • Selling Wearables Video (435986)

Make personalized recommendations.

  • Choose 3 video customers:
    • One who would benefit from a tablet
    • One who would benefit from a wearable
    • One who would benefit from a hotpot
  • Decide how you would recommend this device to the customer.
  • Select a corresponding rate plan, DIGITS, when applicable and determine cost breakdown.
    • Full device price
    • Down payment (assume a well-qualified customer)
    • Monthly EIP payment for 24 months
    • Monthly rate plan charge
    • Discounts, if any

Debrief

  • How much is T-Mobile ONE for tablets – stand alone and existing voice line?
    • Answer located at: T-Mobile ONE Tablet: Postpaid (TI) (425002)
  • For the newest Apple Watch, which plan is required and how much is it?
    • Answer located at: Data with paired DIGITS plans (431515)
  • What did you learn about selling wearables?
  • Ask learners to volunteer to share a customer and the device and plan they chose for that customer.
    • Have them practice presenting their recommendation.
    • Make sure each category of device is represented.
    • Allow as many learners to share as time permits.

Bring It Together Activity

Purpose

This activity will help Mobile Associates practice Explore & Discover by asking discovery questions, and Guide the Purchase by making a personalized phone recommendation.

Instructions

TIME: 10 min setup, 25 min breakouts, 15 min presentations, 10 min debrief

  • Split class into small groups of 3-4 people.
  • Each group will be assigned a product type (tablets, wearables, hotspots). It’s possible a product group will be assigned more than once.
  • Think of as many scenarios as possible, where your product is useful.
  • Discuss and come to a consensus on the top two scenarios.
  • For those two scenarios, think of a verbal cue you may hear from the customer that would open the door for a conversation about the product.
  • Think of an open-ended question you could ask as a follow-up.
  • Example:
    • Scenario: Phone not allowed to be out at work
    • Verbal Cue: I have to wait until my break to check my phone
    • Question: What if you could check your messages without your phone?
  • Choose one person from your group to share your product, verbal cues, and questions with the rest of the class.

Action

Create Teams Delete Teams

Note Depending upon class size, you may have to limit each group to one scenario, verbal cue, and follow-up question, to allow all teams to participate.

Debrief

  • What is the most important thing you learned in this module?
  • What was most challenging about this module?
  • What did you learn that will help you sell more?
