Review

Purpose

The review will enable Mobile Associates (MAs) to prepare for today’s Week 1 Knowledge check and ask questions about any of the week’s topics.

Explain

  • This week we learned about providing the Un-carrier experience.
  • Each topic we covered makes T-Mobile unique in the wireless industry.
  • Next week will be about products and services expertise.
  • You will get the opportunity to learn about our rate plans, devices, value added services, and more!

Instructions

  1. Display the list of topics from the week on the slide.
  2. Ask learners to gather and review their notes for each topic.
  3. Go down the list one by one.
  4. Ask learners what questions they have about each topic.

If participation is low, try asking some knowledge check questions like these:

  • What are our Un-carrier Principles?
  • What are the components of the Interaction Model?
    • The starred behaviors?
  • What is our network rally cry?
  • What are the names of the personal coverage solutions we offer?
    • What’s the difference between them?

Action

Go through the potential questions of the knowledge check. Tokens are intentionally not available during the review.

Knowledge Check

Purpose

The Week 1 Knowledge Check will enable Mobile Associates to:

  • Demonstrate their knowledge of week 1 content.
  • Close knowledge gaps via group discussion.
  • Complete the first step towards Ready! Certification.

Instructions

Timing: 45 min Knowledge Check, 30 min debrief

  • Learners access Un-carrier Academy and take the week 1 Knowledge Check.
  • Allow learners to have their notes open for the Knowledge Check. They may use any resources available to them (C2, T-Nation, etc.) except for their peers.
  • Once complete, learners should note which questions they struggled with, and which of their answers they had to look up and bring those items to the debrief discussion that will follow.
  • If a learner does not pass the Knowledge Check on the first attempt, spend time with the learner reviewing what questions they answered incorrectly prior to their second attempt.

Debrief

  • Which questions were difficult?
  • What did you need to look up?
  • What remaining questions do you have about the content from this week?

Note: If a learner has any remaining questions, leverage the group to teach them what the correct answer is, and where the answer can be found.
