Intro

Purpose

Introduce the impact that a customer’s credit history has on offer eligibility, deposits and down payments. Discuss the importance of having a credit conversation early so that Mobile Associates (MAs) can speak accurately about offers and pricing.

After successfully completing this module, learners will be able to:

  • Define the importance of a credit check early in a sales conversation.
  • Explain how offers may differ based on a customer’s credit history.
  • Practice tactfully suggesting a credit check early in a sales conversation.

Explain

  • A customer’s credit history will determine if they are considered “well- qualified” or “less-qualified”.
  • Well-qualified customers typically qualify for offers that allow them to finance more and pay less out of pocket, upfront.
  • Less-qualified customers will usually be required to pay a larger down payment for devices and accessories, if they are choosing to finance through our equipment installment plan.
  • It’s important to note that overall device and accessory pricing is the same, regardless of credit history.
  • Credit can also determine if a customer is required to pay a deposit to activate service with T-Mobile.
  • Knowing the impact of credit history, it’s a good idea to have the credit conversation early, so you can set accurate expectations on offers and out-of- pocket cost at the point of sale.
  • Credit history can be a sensitive subject for some.
  • Never make assumptions about what a customer will qualify for.
  • Be polite, tactful, and transparent when requesting to check a customer’s credit.
  • Running a credit check mostly applies to new customers. For existing customers, you’ll be able to see their credit eligibility in QuikView or Tapestry.
  • Either way, attempt to determine early in a sales conversation what offers are available to a customer by running a credit check or checking their account in QuikView or Tapestry.

Ask

What’s the risk of spending time understanding a customer’s needs, make a recommendation, and then check their credit?

Possible answers:

  • The customer may feel like they were misled if they find out they are required to pay a large down payment for the device they wanted.
  • The customer may have been really excited about the new device and service they think they’re about to get, only to feel disappointed to have to select something different that fits their budget.
  • Customer may leave without buying if they aren’t able to get the offer they thought they were going to get.

Explain

This

  • Take time to Explore & Discover with a customer interested in signing up for T-Mobile.
  • Identify a comfortable time to ask to check credit eligibility.
  • Be sure to use positive word choice.
  • Checking credit should be a natural part of the conversation flow.

Not That

  • Bringing it up to early in the conversation can make the customer uncomfortable.
  • Using words like ‘bad credit’ can be offensive.
  • No matter what the customer’s credit situation may be, it is our job to find a solution for them that fit’s their needs.

What else is important when having a credit conversation with a customer?

Possible answers:

  • Be transparent, don’t hide the fact that you are running credit.
  • Be patient, some customers want to take more time before running credit.
  • No matter the result of the credit check, be professional and courteous.

Credit Conversations Activity

Purpose

Demonstrate how to make a smooth transition and tactfully ask to run a credit check during a sales conversation.

Action

Demonstrate a credit conversation using scenario one.

  1. Ask for a volunteer to play the customer.
  2. Facilitator will play the Mobile Associate.
  3. Skill practice scenario one.

Now switch it up!

  1. Ask for two volunteers from the class.
  2. This time, the learners will play the role of the Mobile Associate.
  3. Facilitator will play the customer this time.
  4. Provide balanced feedback after each scenario.

Debrief

  • What did you hear that worked well?
  • What, if anything, would you do differently?
  • What are your biggest take-aways?

Explain

Regardless of a customer’s credit outcome, T-Mobile has a plan that meets their needs.

Ask

What are some options when a customer has less than qualified credit?

Sample answers:

  • Smartpick devices
  • T-Mobile Prepaid
  • T-Mobile ONE No Credit Check

Explain

By listening to their needs, taking their credit situation into account and making a personalized recommendation, you can give the customer an Un-carrier Experience and do it the right way!
