Intro

Purpose

At the end of this module, Mobile Associates (MAs) will be able to:

  • Search and find information to give an Un-carrier customer experience based on common issues.
    • Problems with their service.
    • How to use troubleshooting tools.
    • How to practice having the troubleshooting conversation.
    • Buyer’s remorse, returns, and exchanges.
  • Utilize Problem Solved Interaction Model behaviors when empathizing with a customer to resolve customer issues.

Ask

  • Let’s recap your home store visit. Solicit for multiple answers, have fun with the discussion.
  • Before you began your Un-carrier journey to become a MA what were your expectations for the MA role?
  • What specific transactions or interactions did you experience?
    • How did the Mobile Expert (ME) use the Interaction Model throughout the customer interactions?
    • What tools were used?
  • Did anyone observe a ME resolve a customer’s issue during your visit? If so, what did you observe?
  • After spending the day at your home store, how have your expectations changed?

Ask

  • When was the last time you had an outstanding or what we call it, an Un-carrier customer experience?
  • Was the experience handled so well that you told your friends and family about it?
    • NOTE: Based on time available, solicit for multiple answers.

Explain

  • If nothing ever went wrong, then there wouldn’t be an opportunity to give customers an Un-carrier experience. But we all know that’s not the case.
  • Those opportunities to provide an Un-carrier experience will present themselves as an issue or problem the customer is having.
  • These moments can be challenging, because the customer may be upset, impatient or even rude.
  • But when you take the time to be a customer’s expert, show empathy, and help find a resolution to their issue, those challenging situations can lead to the most loyal customers and more sales.

Ask

Which behaviors do you think go the furthest in building customer loyalty when our customers are having issues and why?

Sample answers:

  • Active listening: demonstrates you care and will work to find a resolution.
  • Using tools and resources: provides troubleshooting steps to work towards resolution.
  • Empathizing with the customer: shows you understand and can imagine what they are feeling.

Explain

  • Solving the problem doesn’t always mean you must fix a customer’s device.
  • There may be time when a customer wants a different product or wants to return the purchase.
    • This is where buyer’s remorse, returns and exchanges come into play.

Grand Central

Explain

What is Grand Central?

  • When a customer is having an issue with their service or device, MAs need to know how to help.
  • There are so many different things that could be the root cause of a customer’s issue and we have an amazing tool called Grand Central that takes most of the guesswork out of it for you.
  • Grand Central is a web-based troubleshooting tool to use when problem solving.
  • This tool contains much of the same functionality as Customer Care.
  • MAs can use this information to troubleshoot the most common customer issues.
  • This tool provides information on the customer’s device, account, and network.

Explain

How is it used?

  • Within Grand Central there are several tabs to help with troubleshooting, including the Network and The Fix.
  • The Network provides a deeper dive into the customer’s home coverage and brings awareness of any outages.
  • The Fix is the main troubleshooting flow within Grand Central.
  • Select an issue, and a checklist of actions will appear to remedy the issue.

Explain

How does it improve the customer experience?

  • When Grand Central is fully utilized, customers have a greater chance of leaving our store with their issue resolved, which will lead to higher customer satisfaction.
  • Grand Central contains several WalkMe flows that highlight capabilities such as the ability to walk through The Fix and call out how to navigate through the different troubleshooting steps.

Debrief

  • Which Interaction Model behaviors do you think go the farthest in building customer loyalty when our customers have issues and why?
    • Problem Solved: Listen, show empathy, and apologize.
    • Stay Connected: Set the right expectations.
    • Explore & Discover: Own our network.
  • How can you Make It Personal when finding the root cause of a customers issue?
    • Be your Un-carrier self, let customers know how you would feel being in their shoes.
    • Have a two-way conversation, listen and provide empathy.
    • Spend time on what matters, use Grand Central to get a quick fix.
  • What are the benefits of using Grand Central?
    • By using Grand Central customers have a greater chance of leaving the store with their issue resolved, which will lead to higher customer satisfaction.
    • The ability to walk through The Fix and call out how to navigate through the different troubleshooting steps.
  • How would you describe Grand Central?
    • Web-based troubleshooting tool that all stores have access to.
    • Same troubleshooting tool that Customer Care uses to assist customers and contains much of the same functionality.
    • Provides information on the customer’s device, account, and network, which MAs can use to troubleshoot the most common issues.
  • Where can you find help navigating Grand Central?
    • WalkMe provides many hover-overs and pop-up information that walks Mobile Experts through the key features and functions.
    • It also contains several flows that highlight capabilities, such as the ability to walk through The Fix and calls out how to navigate through the different troubleshooting steps.

Bring It Together Activity

Instructions

Timing: 5 min setup, 1 hr 15 min activity, 10 min debrief

  1. Split class into groups of 3-4.
  2. Learners should review each scenario.
  3. Based on the scenario, learners should practice navigating to the Device Known Issues C2 page to identify how to resolve the customer situation.
  4. Once the solution has been identified, take turns playing the role of the ME demonstrating empathy and explaining the solution.
  5. Rotate until each learner has had a chance to skill practice a scenario.

Scenario 1: Tom

  • Tom has the Samsung J7 Prime.
  • Phone appears to be lagging when trying to navigate from app to app. Tom has a lot of photos on his device of his family that are very important to him.

Scenario 2: Martha

  • Martha has the iPhone X and can’t seem to view her Visual Voicemails.
  • She is frustrated because she didn’t realize she had missed voicemails and calls from important clients.

Scenario 3: Elvis

  • Elvis has the LG GPad X2 8.0 Plus.
  • He’s not sure what he is clicking, but randomly his tablet screen turns black and white. He is frustrated because he doesn’t like watching his Netflix shows in black and white.

Action

Create Teams Delete Teams

Debrief

Scenario 1: Tom

Solution should be to empathize with Tom and check the amount of free memory. If below 150 MB, free up some memory by reviewing applications, photos, and videos to determine which ones Tom would like to remove. Known issues: Samsung J7 Prime (431199)

Scenario 2: Martha

Solution should be to apologize for the issue and let Martha know we’re working with Apple to resolve this as quickly as possible. On the customers iPhone, open the Phone app and tap on Voicemail. At the top left, tap Greeting, then tap Cancel. They should now see the messages and be able to play them. Known issues: iPhone X (435659)

Scenario 3: Elvis

Solution should be to empathize with Elvis and explain the QuikButton key on the right side of the tablet. The QuickButton key toggles the display between full color and grayscale. To revert to full color from grayscale, simple press the QuickButton key again. Known issues: LG GPad X2 8.0 Plus (437171)

Ask

  • What were some of the best empathy statements you heard?
    • ‘Oh no! I’m sure it is frustrating to have your phone continue to switch between Wi-Fi and 4G.’
  • What did you find most challenging being the ME?
    • Taking a moment to empathize and not jumping right into problem solving.
  • Who has a great example of owning an issue?
    • ‘At T-Mobile we have amazing tools that help us troubleshoot these types of issues. Let me pull up Grand Central, this will give us a breakdown of what is going on.’
